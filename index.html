Custom Systems

Not a generic survival setup with a few commands bolted on.

Each system feeds another one. Jobs create the early economy, the market reacts to supply, corporations compete on wealth, cartel selling adds risk, and progression shops give players expensive goals worth chasing.

/drugs /dealer High risk

EcoCartel

Buy seeds from a custom menu, grow contraband outside protected land, process raw product in lab stations, then sell through street dealing. Dealer mode makes you glow, spawns one buyer at a time, and brings the risk of undercover cops or global coordinate leaks.

  • Street dealing is the only way to sell packaged product.
  • Boost zones can pay more for dangerous public selling areas.
  • Top sellers and per-drug leaders feed spawn holograms and Discord alerts.
Live prices Market surges Spawn ticker

EcoMarket

The shop is not static. Sell too much of one item and the price drops for everyone. Quiet items recover over time, while admin-run surges can make copper, iron, or other resources suddenly worth chasing.

  • Reads real shop transactions and updates prices in-game.
  • Market holograms show rising, falling, and surged items.
  • Discord posts turn market moves into server-wide moments.
/corp /gc

EcoCorp

Found a corporation, invite members, use corp chat, share selected company claims, and climb a wealth leaderboard based on member balances.

  • Corp tags appear through PlaceholderAPI/TAB.
  • Corp wealth is tracked for holograms and Discord alerts.
/claims Anti-grief

EcoClaim

Custom land protection designed for a PvP-outside-claims server, with protection against buckets, projectiles, vehicles, mobs, pistons, and common grief routes.

  • Personal claims stay personal.
  • Corp sharing is deliberate, not automatic theft bait.
Tomes I-III Anvil upgrades

EcoMining

Upgrade individual pickaxes with Mining Tomes. Tier I mines vertical trenches, Tier II mines flat 3x3 planes, and Tier III opens a 3x3x3 cube.

  • No Tier IV auto-collect.
  • Hardness and preferred-tool checks block obsidian and ancient debris exploits.
No mob lag Upgradeable

EcoSpawners

EcoSpawners are static loot silos, not entity factories. They generate drops into internal storage while chunks are active, with separate speed and storage upgrades.

  • Creeper, Blaze, and Wither Skeleton are premium sinks.
  • Hoppers can pull output for sorting systems.

Server Economy

Money has routes in, routes out, and public pressure.

The server is built around avoiding the usual week-two inflation collapse. Players can earn through normal play, but major power systems cost serious money and market prices punish over-farming.

Earn Jobs pay for actions Players earn by mining, farming, hunting, fishing, building, and more, with level milestones posted to Discord.
React The market moves Oversold items crash, quiet items recover, and live market surges give players reasons to log in.
Invest Buy progression Mining tomes and EcoSpawners are expensive shop goals rather than admin-only handouts.
Risk Sell contraband Dealer mode pays well, but glowing players, cops, and coordinate leaks create danger.
Compete Leaderboards talk Spawn holograms and Discord alerts highlight top corps, sellers, job progress, and market events.

Player Stories

Designed to create moments people talk about.

EcoCraft’s best systems are not just menus. They create pressure: do you sell now while glowing, hold for a market surge, invest in a spawner, join a corporation, or risk a high-value cartel route?

Cartel exposure

Dealer mode marks you through walls, blocks easy escape routes, and can globally reveal your location if someone grasses.

Corporate competition

Corp wealth, top sellers, playtime, jobs, and money leaderboards are designed to create rivalries without staff needing to force events.

Spawn shops and progression

Player shops, rentable plots, mining tomes, and EcoSpawners give players long-term goals beyond simply building a base and logging out.

Discord integration

Market moves, new corps, cartel busts, mining upgrades, spawner placements, and leaderboard changes can all post as rich Discord alerts.

Core Progression

The server gives players something expensive to want.

Progression is handled through controlled acquisition, not random clutter. Tome shops and spawner shops turn money into capability, while upgrade costs keep late-game cash from becoming meaningless.

All jobs Levels matter Milestone alerts

Jobs without the noise

Jobs are less about intrusive quests and more about rewarding normal play. Players earn from actions, still level up for status and leaderboards, and get subtle visual money feedback while they work.

  • Every player can participate without hard job-slot limits.
  • Level 10, 20, 30 and higher milestones can post to Discord.

Mining Tomes I-III

Tomes are sold through progression shop chests. Players must upgrade in order using an anvil, so powerful mining shapes are earned step by step.

  • Tier I: 3x1 vertical trench.
  • Tier II: 3x3 flat plane.
  • Tier III: 3x3x3 cube excavation.

Launch Plan

Start focused, then add events once the economy breathes.

The strongest launch is not dumping every future idea in on day one. Open with the core economy loop, watch the first week, then use market surges and cartel/corp events to steer player attention.
